The current research has successfully utilized global compositional and structural descriptors (τ, μ, volume) to predict stability and mechanical properties, but it has reached a performance plateau regarding shear modulus (G_VRH) and band gap accuracy. I hypothesize that the "mechanical and electronic bottleneck" is caused by the neglect of local coordination environment distortions and B-site octahedral tilting, which are not fully captured by the Goldschmidt tolerance factor. 

I propose a "Local-Environment Feature Engineering" approach: instead of relying solely on global descriptors, we will compute and incorporate "Octahedral Distortion Indices" (ODI) derived from the existing dataset's structural information. Specifically, we will calculate the variance in B-O bond lengths and the deviation of O-B-O bond angles from the ideal 90°/180° perovskite geometry for the 1283 entries. By integrating these local symmetry-breaking descriptors into the existing hurdle and GPR pipeline, we can better distinguish between cubic, orthorhombic, and monoclinic phases that share similar global radii but exhibit vastly different shear rigidities and electronic band gaps. This will allow the model to move beyond "compositional heuristics" and into "structural-topology-aware" predictions, specifically targeting the improvement of G_VRH regression and the reduction of band gap prediction error for transition metal perovskites.
