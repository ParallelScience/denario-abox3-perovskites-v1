The current research has successfully optimized property prediction and candidate screening using global compositional and structural descriptors. However, the persistent "mechanical gap" (only 17% of data has elastic constants) and the reliance on PBE-level DFT suggest that the model is hitting a ceiling defined by the lack of local bonding information. I hypothesize that the mechanical and thermodynamic properties of ABO3 perovskites are governed by the local coordination environment and the degree of octahedral tilting, which are not fully captured by global descriptors like $\tau$ or `volume`. 

My proposed approach is to implement a **Local-to-Global Graph-based Feature Engineering** layer. Instead of relying solely on scalar descriptors, we will construct a graph representation for each of the 1283 compounds where nodes are atoms and edges represent chemical bonds (A-O and B-O). We will then compute **Local Coordination Fingerprints** (e.g., bond valence sums, bond angle distributions, and octahedral distortion indices) derived from the existing structural data. By integrating these local geometric features into the existing GPR/XGBoost pipeline, we can better distinguish between stable/metastable phases and predict mechanical properties for the 1068 uncharacterized materials by capturing the "local strain" that global descriptors miss. This will specifically address the "Silicate Paradox" and the sensitivity of mechanical stability to structural perturbations, moving beyond simple compositional heuristics to a structure-aware predictive model.
