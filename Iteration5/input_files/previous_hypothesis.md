The current research has successfully utilized global structural and compositional descriptors to predict stability and mechanical properties, but it has largely ignored the "local" chemical environment of the B-site octahedron, which is the primary determinant of perovskite electronic and mechanical behavior. I hypothesize that the performance of the mechanical and electronic models can be significantly improved by incorporating "Octahedral Distortion Descriptors" (ODDs) derived from the existing dataset's structural parameters. Specifically, I propose calculating the B-O bond length variance and the octahedral tilt angle (derived from the deviation of the B-O-B bond angle from 180 degrees, inferred from the crystal system and unit cell volume). By integrating these ODDs—which capture the local symmetry breaking that standard global descriptors like $\tau$ and $\mu$ miss—into the existing hurdle and classification pipeline, we can better resolve the mechanical properties of the 1068 uncharacterized compounds. This approach moves beyond simple composition-based heuristics to a structure-informed feature set that explicitly accounts for the local coordination environment, providing a more granular prediction of the mechanical and electronic landscape without requiring new DFT calculations.
