**Title**: Two-Stage Classification Pipeline for Mechanical Robustness and Thermodynamic Stability in ABO3 Perovskites

**Description**: This research implements a sequential classification architecture to identify high-performance perovskites, bypassing the unphysical noise and extreme sparsity inherent in the 215-sample elastic dataset. The first stage employs a gradient-boosted classifier to predict thermodynamic stability (`is_stable`) across the full 1283-compound set, using structural descriptors (τ, μ, `volume`) and electronegativity differences as primary features. The second stage utilizes a "Mechanical Viability" classifier trained exclusively on physically bounded elastic data—defined by 0 < `K_VRH` < 300 GPa and positive `G_VRH`—to distinguish between mechanically robust phases and dynamically unstable configurations. By treating mechanical viability as a binary classification problem rather than a regression task, the model avoids the catastrophic bias of extreme outliers and negative moduli. This pipeline provides a rigorous filtering mechanism to isolate stable, mechanically sound candidates from the 1068 currently uncharacterized perovskite entries, effectively mapping the viable material space without relying on unreliable numerical predictions for non-existent or unstable phases.
