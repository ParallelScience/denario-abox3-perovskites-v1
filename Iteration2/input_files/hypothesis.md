The current reliance on global structural descriptors (like τ and μ) and static compositional features fails to capture the local coordination environment and electronic hybridization effects that dictate mechanical stability in perovskites. I hypothesize that the "mechanical gap" in the dataset can be bridged by constructing a **Local Coordination Fingerprint (LCF)**, which replaces global geometric proxies with site-specific descriptors: the B-site valence electron count (VEC), the B-O bond valence sum (BVS), and the B-site magnetic moment. By training a **Multi-Task Graph-Regularized Regressor** that simultaneously predicts `K_VRH` and `G_VRH` while using a secondary "physics-informed" loss function to enforce the Cauchy relation ($C_{12} = C_{44}$ for isotropic systems) and the Born stability criteria, we can regularize the model against the noise of the sparse 215-sample elastic subset. This approach moves beyond simple classification by embedding the physical constraints of the perovskite lattice directly into the loss function, allowing the model to interpolate mechanical properties for the 1068 uncharacterized compounds with higher physical fidelity than previous unconstrained regression or binary classification attempts.
